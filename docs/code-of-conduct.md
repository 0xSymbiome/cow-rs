# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as maintainers, contributors, and community members pledge to keep
participation in `cow-rs` respectful, welcoming, and productive for
everyone, regardless of background or experience level. We commit to
acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open and inclusive
community around CoW Protocol tooling.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Offering constructive feedback focused on the technical surface under
  review.
- Respecting differing viewpoints, experiences, and levels of Rust or
  protocol familiarity.
- Gracefully accepting feedback and revising contributions in response.
- Prioritizing what is best for the community and the public Rust SDK
  surface.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, or insults, including through private
  channels.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or
  advances.
- Sustained off-topic or disruptive behavior in issues, pull requests,
  or other project spaces.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in
  a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies inside every project space, including
pull requests, issues, discussions, code review, commit messages,
and any other venue where contributors interact about `cow-rs`. It
also applies when an individual is representing the project in public
spaces.

## Reporting

Report violations through the private channels listed in
[SECURITY.md](../SECURITY.md). The same confidentiality and
response-timing expectations that apply to security reports also
apply to Code of Conduct reports.

If a report concerns broader CoW Protocol community behavior beyond
this repository, follow the escalation path of the CowDAO community
governance channels in addition to the repository-level report.

## Enforcement

Repository maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of
acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair
corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem
inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or
reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other
contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will
communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.
